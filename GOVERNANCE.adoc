== Overview

F19 Stealth Glider is a *single-maintainer project*. This document says so
plainly rather than describing a committee that does not exist. What follows is
therefore short on process and long on the one thing that genuinely constrains
decisions here: the project's design invariants.

If the project gains regular contributors, this document is the first thing that
should change.

== Roles

Maintainer:: Reviews and merges pull requests, cuts releases, triages issues,
and — most importantly — is the only person who may approve a change to a design
invariant. See <<invariants>>.

Contributors:: Anyone opening an issue or pull request. Expected to follow the
link:CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md[Code of Conduct], sign off their commits (DCO 1.1), and
read link:VERIFICATION.md[VERIFICATION.md] before proposing a change to
gameplay or geometry.

The current maintainer is listed in link:MAINTAINERS.adoc[MAINTAINERS.adoc].

[[invariants]]
== The invariants, and why they outrank taste

Most projects govern *code quality*. This one governs *a claim*: that the
cellular automaton is a genuine antagonist rather than atmosphere. That claim is
falsifiable, and it is easy to destroy accidentally — not by writing bad code,
but by making a reasonable-looking change that quietly removes the difficulty's
source.

The following therefore require explicit maintainer approval, and a change to
any of them must be accompanied by a re-run of the full verification ledger:

* *The substrate is B3/S23 and is never special-cased.* No scripted movers, no
  entity tables, no per-cell exceptions.
* *`src/level.mjs` is frozen geometry.* When a route fails verification, the
  legitimate responses are to redesign the crossing or to record the negative
  result. Retuning lane offsets until a test passes is not one of them.
* *The contact rule is not weakened to open a route.* This is the project's
  standing falsifier: if a crossing becomes learnable by feel without reading
  the automaton's phase, the substrate is scenery and the level has failed.
* *`stamp()` throws on out-of-bounds.* It once clipped silently and
  manufactured a result that survived review. Fail-loud is a correctness gate.
* *The licence stays AGPL-3.0-or-later.*

These are recorded machine-readably in
`.machine_readable/contractiles/trust/Trustfile.a2ml` under `trust-deny-areas`,
and several are enforced as hard CI probes by
link:.machine_readable/contractiles/must/Mustfile.a2ml[the Mustfile].

== Decision making

Minor changes:: Documentation, tooling, CI, refactoring that leaves the ledger
unchanged. Any maintainer, no discussion needed.

Substantive changes:: New mechanics, UI changes, engine changes. Discussed in an
issue or PR first. Must state which ledger claims are affected and show the
re-run output.

Invariant changes:: Anything in <<invariants>>. Requires an explicit decision
record appended to `.machine_readable/descriptiles/META.a2ml` (the `D-nn`
series) stating what changed, why, and what evidence settled it. A change here
without a decision record should be reverted regardless of its merits.

== Evidence, not assertion

The project rule is that *a claim without a runnable script is not a result*.
Every row in link:VERIFICATION.md[VERIFICATION.md] cites a script that runs with
plain `node`. A pull request that adds a claim must add the script that
discharges it, and a pull request that changes behaviour must show the affected
scripts re-run — not merely assert that they would pass.

This extends to negative results. `src/verify4.mjs`, `5`, `6`, `7` and `10` are
design searches that found nothing viable. They are kept in the tree, and
excluded from the CI ledger, so that the search space already ruled out stays
visible to the next person. Deleting them would silently invite the same dead
ends to be re-explored.
