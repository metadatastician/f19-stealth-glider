== Areas of responsibility

Because there is one maintainer, ownership is not divided by area. What *is*
divided is the level of scrutiny a change receives:

[cols="2,3", options="header"]
|===
| Area | Review standard

| `src/engine.mjs`
| Highest. Changes to the B3/S23 substrate invalidate the entire verification
  ledger; every cited script must be re-run and its output shown.

| `src/level.mjs`
| Highest. Frozen geometry — see `GOVERNANCE.adoc` §invariants. Requires a
  decision record.

| `src/mission.mjs`
| High. Contains the contact rule, which is the project's standing falsifier.

| `VERIFICATION.md`
| High. A claim may only change alongside a re-run of the script that
  discharges it.

| `src/verify*.mjs`, `src/ui.js`, `src/build.mjs`
| Normal.

| Docs, CI, `.machine_readable/`
| Normal — but a contractile probe that cannot pass against this tree is a
  defect, not documentation.
|===

== Becoming a maintainer

There is no formal ladder yet, which is the honest position for a project this
size. In practice: contribute substantively, demonstrate that you understand why
the design invariants exist rather than merely that they exist, and ask.

The single most useful qualification is a track record of *reporting negative
results accurately* — this project has already had one claim die in testing
because a harness bug produced a false positive, and catching that kind of thing
matters more here than shipping features.
